 in this design please follow the following specifications and use the wiring as a loose guide

 11.1 v battery 2200mAH 
 1000kv motor
 30A esc
 any reciver
 4 servo motors (elevator, rudder, 2x alirons)
 10x5 prop

 mount the battery onto the nose/firewall of the front fuselage with m3 bolts

 connect the three phased wires to the esc according to how you want your motor to spin

 attach the propeller accordingly (do not test with propeller attached)

 connect the esc to the battery and the 3 esc pwm wires to the RX
 in the way you wish the controler to control the motor

 attach the other servo wires to the rx


 and follow your controllers guide on how to connect to the rx and function


 #assmbely

 carefully cut two 1000mm long 4mm thick carbon  fibre rods dirctly through the middle to obtain four 500mm long rods
 attach them to the aft fuselage and the fin and boom connector
 glue one nut to the nut cutout (m3) and glue three nuts to their cutouts on the forward fuselage
 you can now attach the hatch with m3 bolts
 use 2 more 4mm OD 1000mm long carbon fibres as spar and insert them through the holes in the aft fuselage

 insert the ribs as described in the assembly 10cm apart aside from the penultimate rib
 attach a foam board skin to the wings with a glue gun

and leave cutouts for the control surfaces and tape them on with fibre tape

attach 200mm long 4mm tubes of cf to the fin and boom connector and place ribs accordingly. and attach a foamboard skin to that aswell

you can now wire the aircraft and test it before flying.
