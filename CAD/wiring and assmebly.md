 in this design, please follow the following specifications and use the wiring as a loose guide

 11.1 v battery 2200mAH 
 1000kv motor
 30A esc
 any receiver
 4 servo motors (elevator, rudder, 2x ailerons)
 10x5 prop

 mount the battery onto the nose/firewall of the front fuselage with m3 bolts

 connect the three-phase wires to the ESC according to how you want your motor to spin

 attach the propeller accordingly (do not test with propeller attached)

 connect the ESC to the battery and the 3 ESC PWM wires to the RX
 in the way you wish the controller to control the motor

 attach the other servo wires to the RX


 and follow your controller's guide on how to connect to the RX and function


 # assembly

 carefully cut two 1000mm long 4mm thick carbon  fibre rods directly through the middle to obtain four 500mm long rods. 
 attach them to the aft fuselage and the fin and boom connector. Be careful here as carbon fibre is a carcinogen; wear a mask, and cut with a fine saw or preferably something akin to a grinder in running water<img width="1366" height="768" alt="Screenshot (1175)" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4aabea50-8840-42c0-bf14-a70a876f1dfa" />

 glue one nut to the nut cutout on the aft fuselage (m3), and glue three nuts to their cutouts on the forward fuselage.  use 2 more 4mm OD, 1000 mm long carbon fibres as spars and insert them through the holes in the aft fuselage <img width="1366" height="768" alt="Screenshot (1176)" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/22be8546-ea6e-4104-a48b-69a82a6ff36d" />
Please centre them so that around 47 cm is out on each side.

 Insert the ribs as described in the assembly, 10 cm apart aside from the penultimate rib, IE attach each rib from the fuselage at 10 cm apart, but the final smooth rib doesn't have to be; it sits flush with the CF rod
 . Attach a foam board skin to the wings with a glue gun. <img width="1366" height="768" alt="Screenshot (1177)" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4b845579-6306-4dcc-87b3-0b19588f4046" />
The way to attach the ribs is that the normal and longest goes in. After three of those, insert a half-cut fin; after that, insert the half-cut fin with the servo mount, and finally insert the wing tip fin

and leave cutouts for the control surfaces and tape them on with fibre tape after attaching them with servo; leave cutouts for them as well

attach 200mm long 4mm tubes of CF to the fin and boom connector and place ribs accordingly. and attach a foamboard skin to that as well; repeat as is in assembly: one rib at the root, one at the tip, and one in between, the half cut, the smooth, and the servo mounted respectively

you can now wire the aircraft and test it before flying.
